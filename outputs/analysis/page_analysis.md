# Website Analysis

## URL

https://en.wikinews.org/wiki/Main_Page

## Page Title

Wikinews, the free news source — Main Page

## Main Topic

Wikinews is a free, open-content news wiki operated by the Wikimedia Foundation. The main page serves as the homepage and aggregates the latest user-contributed news articles from around the world, organized by recency and topic.

## Page Sections

### Header
- Located at the very top of the page.
- Contains the **Wikinews logo** (globe with pencil icon) on the left, the site name "Wikinews" with the tagline "the free news source."
- Includes a search box and links to user account functions (log in / create account).
- A horizontal navigation menu with links such as Main Page, Latest news, Categories, and more.

### Navigation (Left Sidebar)
- A left-side panel containing navigation links: Main Page, Latest news, Categories, Countries, and more.
- Also includes community links: Community portal, Help, Wikinews on social media, Recent changes, Random article.

### Main Content
- Dominated by a **"Welcome to Wikinews"** heading with the subtext "The free news source you can write."
- The top of the main content area displays the **current date** and a **"Did you know?"** column on the right.
- Features several **lead news stories** with headlines, thumbnail images, and short summaries. Visible stories include:
  - **Australia granting refuge** to five senior domain national soccer team players.
  - **Moreton Bay illegally evicting homeless campers** in Queensland.
  - **Russia publishing its 2025 EU sanctions** changes.
  - **US President Donald Trump** appearing to make threatening comments about Canada.
  - **New Zealand Council plans** for a public consultation on parking near stadiums.
- Below the lead stories, sections for **"Write an article"**, **"Recent Interviews"**, and **"Original Reporting"** that invite community participation.

### Right Sidebar
- **"Did you know?"** section presenting lesser-known news facts with bullet points.
- **"Wikinews Interviews"** section with links to recent interview articles.
- **"Around Wikinews"** box with links to related pages and community resources.
- **"Sister projects"** section linking to other Wikimedia Foundation projects: Wikipedia, Wikibooks, Wiktionary, Wikiquote, Wikisource, Wikiversity, Wikidata, and Commons.

### Footer
- Contains standard Wikimedia footer navigation links: About Wikinews, Disclaimers, Privacy policy, Contact Wikinews, Mobile view.
- **"Welcome in other languages"** section with links to Wikinews editions in Arabic, Catalan, German, Spanish, French, Italian, Japanese, Polish, Portuguese, Russian, Swedish, Chinese, and more.
- **Powered by MediaWiki** badge and Wikimedia Foundation logo.

## Key Elements

### Links
- Numerous article headline links in the main content area pointing to individual news stories.
- Navigation links in the left sidebar and top bar.
- Language edition links in the footer.
- Sister project links in the right sidebar and footer.

### Buttons
- Search button in the header search box.
- "Write an article" call-to-action in the main content area.

### Images
- Wikinews globe-pencil logo in the header.
- Thumbnail news images alongside lead articles (soccer players, homeless camp, Donald Trump, New Zealand flag).

### Forms
- Search form in the header.

## Summary

The Wikinews English Main Page is the homepage of the free, collaborative news website run by the Wikimedia Foundation. The page is structured in a classic wiki layout: a top header with logo and search, a left navigation sidebar, a wide main content column showcasing the latest news articles with images and summaries, and a right sidebar with supplementary information (Did you know, Interviews, Sister projects). The footer provides language options and Wikimedia boilerplate links. The primary purpose of the page is to surface the most recent community-written news stories and invite readers to contribute their own articles. Content spans international news topics including politics, sports, and society.
